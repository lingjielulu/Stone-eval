# Short Story CFPG Candidate Review

## Candidates

### tell_tale_heart:ftp_candidate:000001

- Type: `psychological` / payoff `delayed_revelation`
- Distance: 16 paragraphs
- F P0001: The narrator claims his disease has sharpened his senses, especially his hearing, allowing him to hear all things in heaven, earth, and hell.
- T: The police officers accept the narrator's invitation to sit and rest in the old man's chamber, directly above the concealed corpse. The narrator begins to feel unwell, hears a ringing in his ears, which becomes more distinct and transforms into the sound of a beating heart.
- P P0017-P0018: The narrator hears a heartbeat-like sound that grows louder, believes the police officers also hear it, and finally confesses to the murder, screaming about the beating of the old man's heart.
- Rationale: The acute hearing is prominently introduced in the opening paragraph and explicitly paid off in the climax when the narrator hears the imagined heartbeat, forming a clear delayed setup and payoff with non-trivial narrative distance.

### tell_tale_heart:ftp_candidate:000002

- Type: `psychological` / payoff `literal`
- Distance: 7 paragraphs
- F P0010: When the narrator first hears the old man's heartbeat before the murder, he describes it as 'a low, dull, quick sound, such as a watch makes when enveloped in cotton.'
- T: The police officers' prolonged presence in the chamber, combined with the narrator's growing anxiety and guilt, triggers the auditory hallucination of the heartbeat.
- P P0017: During the police visit, the narrator hears the same sound again, described with the exact same phrase: 'a low, dull, quick sound—much such a sound as a watch makes when enveloped in cotton.'
- Rationale: The exact repetition of the distinctive simile across paragraphs creates a strong textual echo, marking a clear foreshadow-payoff relationship with a non-trivial interval.

### tell_tale_heart:ftp_candidate:000003

- Type: `object` / payoff `literal`
- Distance: 9 paragraphs
- F P0002: The narrator reveals that his sole motive for murder is the old man's 'vulture eye'—a pale blue eye with a film over it—which makes his blood run cold and which he wishes to rid himself of forever.
- T: On the eighth night, when the narrator opens the lantern a tiny bit, the thin ray of light falls directly on the vulture eye, which is wide open. This sight enrages the narrator and directly triggers the murder.
- P P0011: After killing the old man by dragging him to the floor and pulling the bed over him, the narrator examines the corpse, confirms the heart has stopped, and states with satisfaction, 'His eye would trouble me no more.'
- Rationale: The eye as a motive and its resolution are clearly linked, but the payoff is relatively direct and less delayed compared to other candidates. Nonetheless, it forms a recognizable setup and payoff structure with a moderate interval.

## Verification

### tell_tale_heart:ftp_candidate:000001

- accepted: `True`
- rationale: The narrator's early boast of heightened hearing (F) directly sets up the later auditory hallucination of the heartbeat (P). The trigger—police sitting over the body and the narrator's growing paranoia—explains the timing. This is a classic foreshadowing structure: a character trait introduced early is later instrumental in the climax, and reading P retroactively reinterprets F as a deliberate warning of the narrator's sensitive auditory perception leading to his downfall.
