# Short Story CFPG Candidate Review

## Candidates

### last_leaf:ftp_candidate:000001

- Type: `object` / payoff `delayed_revelation`
- Distance: 24 paragraphs
- F P0030-P0031: Behrman, a failed artist, has always talked about painting a masterpiece but never started; a blank canvas has been waiting for 25 years.
- T: On the stormy night when the last real leaf falls, Behrman goes out in the rain to paint a replacement leaf, leading to his pneumonia and death; the trigger is observable the next morning when his wet clothes and painting tools are found.
- P P0054-P0055: After Behrman dies, Sue reveals that he painted the last leaf on the wall during the storm, calling it his masterpiece.
- Rationale: The blank canvas and repeated mention of an unstarted masterpiece are a classic object/ promise setup, explicitly paid off when the painted leaf is called his masterpiece.

### last_leaf:ftp_candidate:000002

- Type: `psychological` / payoff `delayed_revelation`
- Distance: 34 paragraphs
- F P0020-P0022: Johnsy becomes fixated on the ivy leaves, counting them down and asserting that when the last one falls, she will die.
- T: The stormy night when the last real leaf falls, and Behrman, knowing Johnsy's belief, paints a leaf to make her think it's still there; observable trigger: the next morning, the leaf is still there after the storm, and later, the discovery of the lantern and paint.
- P P0054-P0055: After Johnsy recovers, Sue reveals that the last leaf was actually a painting by Behrman, which stayed on the wall despite the wind, preventing Johnsy from giving up.
- Rationale: Johnsy's superstition is the central psychological driver; the payoff reveals the hidden mechanism that subverted her belief, making it a strong delayed setup.

### last_leaf:ftp_candidate:000003

- Type: `retrospective` / payoff `misdirection`
- Distance: 15 paragraphs
- F P0040: The last ivy leaf is described with unusual detail, appearing both fresh and decayed, and remains impervious to the storm.
- T: As the last real leaf falls during the storm, Behrman paints a fake one. The leaf's failure to move or fall in the wind is noticed when Johnsy demands the shade be raised.
- P P0055: Sue reveals that the leaf was painted by Behrman, explaining its strange appearance and persistence.
- Rationale: The description is subtle and only clearly signals the deception in retrospect; it may be missed on first reading, but it qualifies as a planted clue.
